Prologue · प्रस्तावना

Behind the Veil is Jest

What is it that you fear, that lies at the bottom of your self? Is it a version of yourself that you detest, that you could never conceive to be true? Or is it an amalgamation of taboo, forbidden vignettes that scar the beauty of your untainted, polished mask, glistening for all to see?

Why do we blindfold the Jester, that prances around the mindscape, running with a tongue of scissors and a taped mouth, when all that he has to offer is a comedic sincerity?

The Sutra of the Grinning Jester · विदूषक सूत्र

The highest heathen,

wears abstinence on his cheek,

but I feast instead.

In a blurry elated high, he meanders outside to the Garden of Eros — his beloved Maithunya (मथैन्युय). Riddled with petals of a myriad shades, the nude afterglow of flowers scream the call of a hundred Sirens.

The Jester, maniacally tosses another flower corpse into the sky, crumbled jasmine unfurling from his clenched fist. The petals quietly land upon the ground, cradled by the sob of a seraph.

Brutality is the highest form of humor.

Yet, a mystical red rose, stands silently in the centre of the Garden, without wailing nor making a sound. Silently. The Jester, with hungry hands, lunges his fist at the beautiful thing. Oozing blood is the outcome, leaking from his fingers.

The Hedonic Shrine

A rotting rafflesia swallows the illusion of the Garden, its colossal petals hovering over the Jester, obscuring the clouds.

  • Bottles of spilled wine
  • Ashed cigarette butts
  • Apsaras clad in a string of cloth
  • Decaying chunks of meat and piles of fish bones
  • Wasted grains of rice, strewn into wet, old clumps

Profanity Petals I  ·  अपवित्रता

Debauchery

व्यभिचार  ·  लालफल

I tried to find God,

in the cleavage of a woman.

I found his silhouette,

in the echo of a moan,

the softness of a caress,

in between her squeals of joy,

and within the folds of her tender flesh.

Yet, his fullness remained elusive,

and in completion, I was empty.

Profanity Petals II

Mad Merlot

मद्य का पागलपन

I tried to find God,

in the bottom of a bottle.

The first sip was the worst,

bitter, burning, unpleasant,

yet the soothing blur,

was the bed indulgence awoke from,

stoking the napalm,

of relentless pursuit,

yet a false image meanders,

spouting nonsense,

mistaking a sedation with the stench of rot,

for the divine.

Profanity Petals III

Asphyxiated Camels

श्वास-अवरुद्ध ऊँट

I tried to find God,

at the end of a cigarette.

Endlessly philosophy,

romanticizing him,

was weaved like an orchestra,

painting a magnum opus,

out of carefree, floating nicotine pastel,

yet in the dissipating head rush,

there was only dissonance,

silence,

then emptiness.

Profanity Petals IV

The Honey Pool of Desire

इच्छा का मधु कुण्ड

The pool of honey,

named Desire,

promises satiation,

but in the fine print,

is detailed,

the diabetic daydream,

and a stagnant stickiness,

that our hunger,

wilfully ignores.

Just one more bite.

Profanity Petals V

An Orchestra of Black Daffodils

काले डैफोडिल का वाद्यवृंद

All exuberance and terror,

dances on your lily flavored fingernails,

ethereal burial of all our disturbances,

into whatever flesh there is to hold,

and from the centre of our eyes,

unfurls a million blooming lotus flowers,

the nectar of which,

when drunk fully,

dries into dark, black daffodils,

in the numbness of which,

we lie,

wondering if the promises,

of this fleeting euphoria,

bleeds into something else.

Farewell,

until tomorrow,

when we shall do it,

all again.

Cherry Blossom

Profanity Petals IX

Emperor Peasant

सम्राट किसान

I drape myself,

with the suave of an Emperor,

sauntering with soliloquies,

spewed with hesitation none,

undone swooning swan necks,

choked between loving hands.

Yet after her gasping,

and a final kiss on the cheek,

the evaporated high,

condenses, densely,

reminding myself,

that distraction strangulation,

only suffocates purpose,

asphyxiating his generosity,

with a puddle of pleasant memories

and nothing else.


I choose to barter,

this imaginary saliva kingdumb,

for a higher Rasa,

that calls to me.


This is the last time,

I promise,

To this love,

I am but a peasant.

Profanity Petals X

Runner's High

धावक का उत्साह

Run on the hedonic treadmill,

sweating bullets of pleasure,

diamond beads, suspended in air

perfect for a fleeting moment,

to recognize that

the price of pleasure's peak,

is the plateau of the soul.

Maybe one more sprint,

vigorously so,

before I turn the thing off.

Sacred Embers I  ·  पवित्र अंगारे

God as a Knife

ईश्वर एक चाकू की तरह

The twisting, blood soaked knife,

meanders within the tender ribcage,

ever so slightly scraping,

initials of pain into the bone,

and whirling in a frenzy,

into the unprotected tender heart.

Yet,

even the atrocity of this blade,

has a golden hilt,

that screams of an OHM,

and wails a wet bead of growth,

scented of Kali.

Sacred Embers II

Jasmine

चमेली

Led by pull

of external sensations,

longing for touch,

burdened by the echoes,

of a lonesome breath,

yet equally draped

is a solemn irony —

The perfect jasmine,

freshly and eternally bloomed,

exists already within the one,

that seeks it,

and in its realization,

is made aware the garden,

of its floral mystique.

Sacred Embers III

Motion Sickness

गति बीमारी

Then there's the duality,

seductive, dancing temptress,

pirouette from stress to bliss,

tip toeing between the lines,

of morality and momentary immortality,


In both its qualities,

of cigarettes smoked in a prayer room,

unprotected sex in a shrine,

swear words in a sermon,

and an adventurous nun in the confession booth,

there lies both the beauty,

and the devastating imperfection,

that being human brings.


Attach yourself to neither,

but do not forget,

to enjoy the absurdity,

as this life,

is a gift of ups and downs,

as long as you manage,

the motion sickness,

of change.

Sacred Embers VIII

Dhyana

ध्यान

I tried to find God,

in meditation.

The thoughts began to pummel,

my innocent, unprepared Spirit,

Sacred Embers IX

Hatha Halcyon

हठ शान्ति

Then I stopped,

trying to find God,

but began becoming,

a beacon for his visit,

as energetic brightness,

purified and present,

stillness embodied,

personified mysticism.

In the lack of seeking,

he arrived,

and then I realized,

he was never hiding,

but only waiting to be seen —

within.

Sacred Embers XI

Zenfaced

ज़ेन मुख

In the solitude of an orange cotton mat,

the memories of a distant lifetime flash —

a flowing river, reflects the emptiness

through echoing laughter laced with cheap red wine,

acidic arousal and intoxication

accentuated with the hedonic confetti — a cigarette

hanging from my cracked lips.


Some girl, yet to be a woman

and I, the inner kingdom usurped by childishness,

meander around our desire

partially indulgent, totally lost.


Yet,

in one conscious inhalation,

sunlight consumed voraciously,

yoga drunk from the divine

and the curtailed, still pond of the mind,

Transcendence is the drug,

no longer numb, but fully feeling —

ZENFACED.

Epilogue · Tandava · ताण्डव

Tandava

ताण्डव — The Dance of Shiva

When I look up,

There is not light,

but an effulgent darkness,

to which I am carried

on the slippery back of a Cobra.


From its flickering tongue,

Steps out a being,

That I recognize as Shiva.


The golden trident, gleams with a quieter glow

His entire body coated in a dark azure blue,

Borders of a deep red adorning his third eye.


He dances with utmost ferocity,

Tongue out, dreadlocks tempestuously lashing into the air,

Havoc, Chaos and Destruction,

All bowing down in adoration.


I stand unmoving, eyes closed, absorbed.


The violence stops.


From the remnants of destruction,

emerges a floral tapestry —

petals of a deep red rose,

fall in a slow, careless motion,

cradled by the palms of Shakti

too delicate for the buzzing ground.


A single tear of Shiva,

leaks from his eye,

forming a pool of water,

upon which the petals swim.


In the reflection

Of the rose water,

I see myself

Stripped away

But whole.

The Rebellion of Breath

श्वास का विद्रोह

Inhale primordial chaos,

exhale liberation's smoke,

and savor the scent in between,

of the universe twirling itself,

from mystery to miracle.

The Dramatis Personae · पात्र

The Souls of the Garden

🃏 The Jester विदूषक

The unhealthy mystic who leans toward self-destruction. He feasts on indulgence wearing pompousness like a birthmark, until the Garden devours him. The jester sutra: comedy in the face of tragedy.

🌹 Maya माया — Illusion

A beautiful woman who is illusion incarnate. She appears with the perfume of a thousand gardens, eyes holding the naked full moon. She offers the Jester everything — and dissolves into a crystal of reflection.

👁 The Gut Cyclops पेट का चक्षुश — Indulgence

A manifestation of desire and craving. He stands at the gate, razor sharp teeth protruding from intestinal walls, adorned with the heads of those who faltered at his stare. He is conquered only by stillness.

🔥 Agni अग्नि — Sacred Fire

The dying flame that the Wanderer tends. His fire dims with every indulgence and burns brighter with every honest reckoning. He becomes the final companion — and ultimately dwells within the Mystic's own breath.

⚡ Shakti शक्ति — Divine Power

She arrives in a celestial chariot, the melted moon coiled in her hair. She offers the Enlightened One a final test — the chalice, then her naked body. The portal to the other world rests within her, and she is also Maya.

🕉 The Mystic रहस्यवादी — The Arrived One

What the Jester becomes after the mask shatters, the Cyclops is faced, and Agni is tended. Gray-haired, unkempt but wise. A lotus floats above his head. His third eye opens. He walks toward Shiva. He understands.

The Lotus Sutras is a book of profanity and prayer — two sides of the same OM. It follows a single consciousness through the complete arc of the tantric path: indulgence, confrontation, dissolution, and the terrifying clarity of what remains.

The Jester is not the villain. He is the self that has not yet faced itself — witty, excessive, genuinely charming, ultimately hollow. His mask does not crack until Maya, the ultimate illusion, scratches through it during the most intimate act possible.

What emerges is the Wanderer — wounded, real, standing before a dying fire called Agni with the question: will you tend this flame, or go back for one more bottle?

The book draws from the Panchamakara of Tantra (the five sacred transgressions), the Bhagavad Gita's instruction on desire and detachment, and the oldest observation in mysticism: the same appetite that destroys you, directed inward, becomes the fire that illuminates.

The Profanity Petals and Sacred Embers are not opposites. They are the same garden, seen from different eyes.
